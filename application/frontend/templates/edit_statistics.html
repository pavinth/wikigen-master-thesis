Tab description (click to expand)

Press Generate Editing Statistics to generate editing statistics. Editing statistics provide data about the distribution of revisions and editors for the chosen article over time. The data can be for example used in order to identify patterns related to effects of external events on the phenomenon under study.


Select time frame for the analysis. You can leave time frame selection fields blank to analyse the entire period, for which data on Wikipedia exist.

Edits statistics section consists of three parts:

First part (Yearly revisions statistics): yearly data for the distribution of edits (Details in the corresponding section).

Second part (Detailed revisions statistics): detailed view on revisions distribution on a monthly basis (Details in the corresponding section).

Third part (Edits statistics: measures): measures for the whole period of time such as the overall amount of revisions existing for an article.

Every part has its own section which can be opened or closed by clicking on it.

This section contains a graph representing the amount of revisions over all years the article existed on the Wikipedia platform.

Every revision is an edit of an article. But not every edit is a revision. Several revisions can be considered as one edit.

There are three different types of edits you can choose from:

Overall: all existing revisions of the article

Distinct: all revisions of the article where a sequence of chronologically succeeding revisions by the same author is counted as one edit

Major distinct: distinct edits according to the above definition excluding all minor edits. A minor edit is a flag on a revision which is set by the user to signalize that no significant changes were made to the article. See definition of minor edit here: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Minor_edit

The option Combined will show all three different revision types: overall, distinct and major distinct.

No bots option, when chosen, will remove all revisions made by bots for any of the chosen revision type. See definition of bots here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Bots

When a revision type is selected, the corresponding data will be displayed in the graph.

Clicking on any data object in the graph will call a tooltip window displaying the exact value for the data object.

The graph contained in this section provides a more detailed view on the revisions distribution by including monthly data.

Monthly data is provided for all three types of edits (overall, distinct and major distinct)

No bots option: when chosen, will remove all revisions made by bots for all edit types.

The graph is interactive. You can choose the time frame of your interest by either selecting the corresponding range (mouse click and drag) on the actual graph or on the small overview graph below the actual graph. Use overview graph too zoom out.

Clicking on any data object in the graph will call a tooltip window displaying the exact value for the data object.

Edits statistics section consists of three parts:

First part (Yearly editors statistics): yearly data for the distribution of editors (Details in the corresponding section).

Second part (Detailed editors statistics):detailed view on editors distribution on a monthly basis (Details in the corresponding section).

Third part (Editors statistics: measures): measures for the whole period of time such as the overall amount of distinct editors who worked on the article.

Every part has its own section which can be opened or closed by clicking on it.

This section contains a graph representing the amount of editors over all years the article existed on the Wikipedia platform.

There are four different types of editors you can choose from:

Overall: all distinct editors who used to edit the article

Registered: all distinct editors who made edits using a registered Wikipedia account

Anonymous: all distinct editors who made edits anonymously without using an account

Bots: all distinct bot editors. Note that all bots are registered. See definition of bots here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Bots

The option Combined editors will show all four different editor types: overall, registered, anonymous and bots.

When an editor type is selected, the corresponding data will be displayed in the graph.

Clicking on any data object in the graph will call a tooltip window displaying the exact value for the data object.

The graph contained in this section provides a more detailed view on the distribution of editors by including monthly data.

All four types of editors are indicated in the graph.

No bots option, when chosen, will remove all revisions made by bots for all revision types.

The graph is interactive. You can choose the time frame of your interest by either selecting the corresponding range (mouse click and drag) on the actual graph or on the small overview graph below the actual graph. Use overview graph too zoom out.

Clicking on any data object in the graph will call a tooltip window displaying the exact value for the data object

Edits per editor section consists of two parts:

First part(Edits\Editors Graph): a graph showing the monthly and yearly data for the amount of edits per editor (Details in the corresponding section).

Second part(Edits\Editors Ratios): ratios showing edits per editor for different types of edits\editor combinations such as total amount of edits for per anonymous editor.

Every part has its own section which can be opened by clicking on it.

This section contains a graph which shows the number of distinct major edits per user over time.

Note that the number of distinct major edits in the corresponding time period is not divided by the total amount of users in the same period of time. Instead, only those users who made those major distinct edits are considered.

The graph is interactive. You can choose the time frame of your interest by either selecting the corresponding range (mouse click and drag) on the actual graph or on the small overview graph below the actual graph. Use overview graph too zoom out.

Clicking on any data object in the graph will call a tooltip window displaying the exact value for the data object

This section contains a graph which displays all revisions available for the article on a time axis. Every revision is represented as a gray thin vertical line crossing the time axis at the time point where the corresponding revision was made. This graph has two aims:

1) Indicating periods of time where the most revisions were made and gaps in time within which no revisions less made.

2) Navigation between revisions by allowing to click on any revision which will switch to the article section displaying the content of the corresponding historical revision.

Note that given a high number of revisions the whole time frame might appear as a completely filled area. You will have to zoom in order to see distinct revisions.

To use the navigation functionality you have to find the revision of your interest first by using interactive graph, zooming in and hovering the mouse over the points located on the top of each vertical line corresponding to a revision. It will display the date of the revision. If you have found the revision of your interest, click on the point. The article tab will open with the corresponding historical content of the article.

The graph is interactive. You can choose the time frame of your interest by either selecting the corresponding range (mouse click and drag) on the actual graph or on the small overview graph below the actual graph. Use overview graph too zoom out.

The graph include monthly data for edits per editor statistics.
