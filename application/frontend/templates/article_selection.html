To select an article you need to enter the exact name of a Wikipedia article.

There are three ways to find out the exact case-sensitive name of the article:

1) The surest way to find out the exact name of the Wikipedia article is to open the article in the browser and look at the link displayed in the address field. The name of the article is the last part of the link after the last “/”. See screenshot below.


2) When you browse through Wikipedia directly or within the application you can always click on any internal Wikipedia link and copy the address as it is shown on the screenshot below.


3) Guess the correct article name. Most Wikipedia articles have the same name as the subject of the article. For example “Barack Obama” is the exact name of the Wikipedia article for the U.S. president. Nevertheless, some articles have non-trivial names. For example, the exact name for the article devoted to Apple corporation is “Apple Inc.

After you have copied the link you can paste it into the WikiGen search field and remove the first part of the link leaving the exact name of the article

After you have selected the article, WikiGen applicaton will start. It will display the Wikipedia article and allow switching between different functionalities the application provides.
